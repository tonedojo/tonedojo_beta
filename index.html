How to answer
1. Press Play to hear a tone (or interval, or chord).
2. Drag a stone from below the staff onto the note you heard. For dyads and chords, place all stones before submitting.
3. Press Submit to check your answer. Correct answers advance the bar · too many wrong resets it.
The glowing rod above the staff tracks your momentum — it moves right as you answer correctly and resets if you fall behind. Fill it to advance to the next rank.
Training starts in C major. Key transposition is available via the 🔑 button above the staff once you're underway.

Understanding Your Stats
Overview
Total answers — how many questions you've submitted this session.
Correct — how many were right on the first try.
Accuracy — correct ÷ total. Green means ≥ 75%, which is the threshold to advance.
Best streak — your longest run of consecutive correct answers.
Accuracy by note / interval / chord
A bar for each scale degree you've been tested on, showing how often you got it right. Green ≥ 75% · amber ≥ 50% · red below 50%. Labels are sargam syllables — Sa (Do), Re, Ga (Mi), Ma (Fa), Pa (Sol), Dha (La), Ni (Ti). Short bars mean you need more practice on that degree.
Most confused
The pairs you mix up most often, ranked by frequency. "Heard Sa+Pa → answered Sa+Ma" means you heard those two degrees but placed the stones as if the upper note were Ma instead of Pa. For melodic intervals, arrows show order: "Sa→Pa" means Sa first, then Pa. These are your specific weak spots — the game already weights future exercises toward them automatically.
Confusion matrix
A grid where rows are what you actually heard and columns are what you answered. The diagonal (top-left to bottom-right) shows correct answers — darker cells off the diagonal show where you're confusing one item for another. A hot cell at row M3, column P4 means you often answer "perfect 4th" when the correct answer is "major 3rd."
How to Play
The basics
Press Play to hear a tone. Drag the black stone from below the staff onto the note you heard, then press Submit. For exercises with two or more notes, place all stones before submitting.
Ranks
Rank numbers run from 41 (beginner) downward — lower is harder. Each rank introduces new notes, intervals, or chords. Answer accurately and you advance; too many wrong answers and you reset to the beginning of the current rank.
The energy bar
The bar tracks your momentum. Fill it to advance to the next rank. Let it empty and you reset.
Solfège & Sargam
After a correct answer, the note is sung back to you in solfège (Do Re Mi…) and then in sargam (Sa Re Ga…), the Indian equivalent. These can be toggled on or off with the Do Re Mi and Sa Re Ga buttons.
Etude mode
Two listening modes, no answering required. 🎵 Warmup plays a through-composed sequence — scale up and down, then I, IV, V triads in all inversions — first in solfège, then in sargam. Use it to orient your ear to the current key before drilling. 🎵 Etude plays a continuous random sampling of objects within the current rank with labels visible on the staff. Use it to absorb the sound-to-syllable relationship passively.
Note colors
Each note is assigned a color of your choosing — pick something that feels right or memorable. Colors are yours permanently: they tint the staff background to the current tonic, fill the stones on reveal, and carry over across sessions. The idea is to give each pitch a sensory identity beyond its name.
